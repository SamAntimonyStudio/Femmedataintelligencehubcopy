Purpose

Dedicated product development intelligence screen. Uses competitor data, internal sales performance, trend signals, and customer feedback to inform new season product development. Turns market research into actionable range planning inputs.

Fashion product development follows a structured cycle: market research → concept development → portfolio strategy → sampling → production. This dashboard supports the first two phases and feeds insights directly into range planning decisions.

Product Development Intelligence Framework

The R&D dashboard applies three lenses to product research:

1. The 80/20 Portfolio Test: Which 20% of SKUs deliver 80% of revenue? Double down on performers, trim underachievers. Femme's dresses maintain 72% fit satisfaction and 78% quality satisfaction — this is the hero category to protect and expand.

2. Core vs. Seasonal Balance: Stable, margin-rich core items (Femme Basics, best-selling knitwear) alongside trend-driven capsule drops every 4–6 weeks.

3. Cannibalisation Check: Watch for overlapping SKUs that compete with each other instead of growing the category.

Strategic Insight: New Season Product Development

For mid-market women's fashion brands like Femme, best practice product development combines three inputs: (1) internal sales analytics (what sold, what returned, what restocked fastest), (2) competitive product intelligence (what competitors are launching, at what price, in what categories), and (3) trend forecasting (social velocity, search trends, seasonal themes). This dashboard unifies all three into one view so the buying and design team can make faster, data-backed range decisions instead of relying on intuition alone. The 4–6 week micro-collection cadence outlined in the GTM aligns with the drop-culture strategy — this dashboard provides the intelligence layer to decide what each drop should contain.

AI Recommendations System — Actionable

Every AI insight must be paired with an action pathway. The recommendation system splits into two modes: Opportunity (green) for good performance to capitalise on, and Alert (terracotta) for underperformance to address.

Opportunity Mode — "Double Down"

When AI detects strong performance: "Instagram Reels CTR is 4.2%, 68% above benchmark. Recommendation: Increase Reels budget by 30% and replicate top-performing format across 3 additional product categories."

Action buttons:

→ [Adjust Budget] opens campaign budget adjustment UI
→ [Email Attention Experts] pre-populates email to agency with insight + recommended action
→ [Create Campaign] jumps to Campaign Planning with pre-filled parameters
→ [Dismiss] with note field

Alert Mode — "Investigate & Adjust"

When AI detects underperformance: "Google Shopping ROAS has dropped 22% WoW. Top 3 Shopping campaigns showing CPM increase with no CVR improvement. Recommendation: Pause lowest-performing ad group, reallocate to brand search."

Action buttons:

→ [Email Salt & FuseLab] pre-populates email with insight, data snapshot, and recommended adjustments

Reverse Brief Wizard — Multi-Step Flow

A 6-step wizard that allows the marketing team to reverse-brief content needs into the AI agent, receive a structured brief, approve it, and have assets and campaign materials auto-generated. Example sequence uses Mother's Day 2026.

1

Input — Campaign Parameters

Pop-up form with fields: Campaign Name, Campaign Period (date range), Campaign Objective (dropdown: Awareness / Conversion / Engagement / Retention), Target Audience, Hero Products (multi-select from Shopify), Budget Range, Channels Required (checkboxes: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, Email, Google Ads), Asset Types Needed (checkboxes: Photography, Video, Carousel, Stories, Email Template, Google Ad Creative), Key Message / Theme (text area), Brand Voice Notes.

Example: "For Her, From Us" — Mother's Day. 21 Apr – 11 May. Conversion + Gifting. Hero products: Kalinda Abstract Dress, Luxe Linen Wrap Top. Budget $12K. All channels. Need Reels, carousels, email sequence, Google Shopping creative.

2

AI Brief Generation

AI processes the inputs and generates a comprehensive content brief. Includes: campaign narrative, channel-by-channel content plan, copy direction per platform, visual mood references, posting schedule, email sequence outline, Google Ads copy variants, and KPI targets per channel. The brief is displayed in a reviewable card layout within the wizard.

3

Sign-Off

Marketing manager (Mel) reviews the AI-generated brief. Options: Approve, Request Changes (with notes field — sends back to Step 2 for AI revision), or Reject. Approval triggers Step 4. The brief is locked and timestamped.

4

Asset Generation

AI produces visual asset mock-ups using real product imagery pulled from femmeconnection.com.au. Generates:

→ 3x Instagram Reels concepts (title + hook + visual direction + caption)
→ 2x Instagram carousels (slide-by-slide copy + image placement)
→ 2x TikTok POV scripts (hook, body, CTA, audio suggestion)
→ 1x Pinterest gift guide board layout
→ 3x Email designs for Klaviyo (Gift Guide / Last Chance / Thank You) with subject lines, preview text, body copy, and CTA hierarchy
→ 4x Google Ad copy variants (headlines, descriptions, sitelinks)

All copy, targeting, timing, and campaign goals shown together in one view.

5

Final Sign-Off

Review all generated assets in a gallery view. Approve individual assets, request revisions on specific items, or approve all. Each asset has: Preview, Copy (copy-to-clipboard), Download, and Edit buttons. Notes field for each asset for production team handoff.

6

Deliver / Post

Distribution panel. Options per channel:

→ Email assets: "Send to Antimony" (populates email with Klaviyo assets attached)
→ Meta assets: "Send to Attention Experts" (populates email with creative brief + assets)
→ Google assets: "Send to Salt & FuseLab" (populates email with ad copy + creative direction)
→ Internal: "Send to Raja" (photography brief with shot list)
→ Organic: "Schedule in Content Calendar" (adds to organic posting schedule)
→ Export All: Downloads complete campaign pack as ZIP
